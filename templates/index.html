📦 Item A → Item B

Association rule: customers who buy Item A are also likely to buy Item B.

📈 Support

Fraction of all orders containing both items. Higher = more common co-occurrence.

🎯 Confidence

P(B | A): probability of buying B given A was bought. Higher = more reliable rule.

🚀 Lift

How much more likely B is bought with A vs. by chance. Lift > 1 = positive association.

💸 Loss Leader

A low-priced Item A with a strong rule toward Item B. Retailers discount A to drive traffic and capture margin on B.

⭐ Loss Leader Score

Composite: (confidence × lift × units_sold) / (avg_price + 1). Higher = stronger candidate.

📢 Suggested Recitation Summary:
This system applies the Apriori algorithm to Olist e-commerce transaction data, treating each customer order as a market basket and each product category as an item. It discovers categories frequently bought together and measures relationship strength using support (frequency), confidence (reliability), and lift (strength above chance). Low-priced antecedent categories that participate in strong rules are flagged as possible loss-leader candidates. Six interactive visualizations — numbered 1 to 6 — let analysts explore patterns from raw frequency to relationship topology to multi-metric comparison.
